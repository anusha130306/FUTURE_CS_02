# FUTURE_CS_02
Task 2 Complete: Phishing Email Detection & Awareness System | Cyber Security Internship
<br>
As part of this task, I analyzed 4 real-world simulated phishing email scenarios — ranging from fake bank alerts and fraudulent job offers to malicious delivery notifications and spoofed password reset emails. For each sample, I performed a structured investigation covering:
1. Email Header Analysis — Checking SPF records, Return-Path mismatches, and verifying sending IP addresses against official servers.
2. Domain & Link Inspection — Identifying lookalike domains (e.g., hdfc-secure-alert.com vs. hdfcbank.com) and suspicious TLDs like .xyz and .net used to deceive users.
3. Phishing Indicator Identification — Spotting red flags like generic greetings, urgency tactics, unexpected attachments, and requests for payment or credentials.
4. Risk Classification — Categorizing each email as High Risk or Medium-High Risk based on analysis findings.
<br>
- Key Takeaways from the Analysis:
 Fake Bank Alert — Urgency + suspicious .xyz domain = Classic phishing playbook. Risk: HIGH
 Fake Job Offer — Asking for ₹3,500 processing fee with no interview process. No legitimate company ever charges recruitment fees. Risk: HIGH
 Fake DHL Delivery — Malware-laced .zip attachment disguised as an invoice. Never open unknown compressed files. Risk: HIGH
 LinkedIn Password Reset — Slight domain modification (linkedin-security.com) designed to steal credentials. Risk: MEDIUM-HIGH
<br>
- Employee Awareness Guidelines I Developed:
Beyond detection, I created actionable, non-technical guidelines to help employees protect themselves:
<br>
Always verify the sender's actual domain — display names can be faked.
Hover over links before clicking to preview the real destination URL.
Never use links in emails to log in — always type the official URL directly into your browser.
Treat urgency as a red flag, not a reason to act fast.
Enable 2FA on all critical accounts.
Legitimate companies never ask for payment or OTPs via email.
